In Depth

Why the Corrugated PP Slip Sheet Is the Smarter Pallet Layer Divider

In any high-velocity distribution operation, the components that keep stacked product separated and stable matter more than most people realize. The tier sheet — the flat panel placed between each layer of stacked cases or containers on a pallet — is one of the unsung workhorses of pallet engineering. Get it right and you reduce product damage, improve stacking consistency, and protect load integrity across a long supply chain. Get it wrong and you deal with collapsed layers, contaminated product surfaces, and a constant stream of reordering single-use consumables.

For decades, the default choice was corrugated cardboard tier sheets. Cheap at the unit level, widely available, and familiar — cardboard became the standard. But the hidden costs and operational drawbacks of single-use cardboard have become increasingly difficult to ignore as operations scale, as food safety standards tighten, and as sustainability programs demand accountability for what gets thrown away. The corrugated polypropylene slip sheet from Elipacko exists precisely to answer all of those concerns.

Twin-Wall Polypropylene: What It Means and Why It Matters

The construction of a PP corrugated slip sheet is fundamentally different from a cardboard tier sheet, and those structural differences explain every performance advantage it holds. Twin-wall polypropylene corrugated sheeting consists of two flat polypropylene face sheets connected by internal vertical ribs — a structure that creates a series of enclosed air channels running the full length of the panel. This geometry distributes compressive load across the entire surface, resists point-load deformation, and eliminates the soft spots and inconsistencies that develop in cardboard sheets after even a single use.

Because polypropylene is a non-porous thermoplastic, the sheet does not absorb water, oils, cleaning solutions, or any liquid that contacts it in a warehouse, production, or cold storage environment. Wet docks, morning condensation cycles, refrigerated storage areas, and even produce environments with active moisture — none of these degrade a PP slip sheet the way they degrade cardboard in a matter of hours. The sheet can be pulled from service, washed, allowed to dry, and returned to the pallet rotation without any loss of structural integrity.

Fiber Contamination: A Risk That Disappears with PP

One of the most underappreciated risks of cardboard tier sheets is fiber shedding. When cardboard flexes, compresses, or gets wet, it releases paper pulp fibers. In food processing and food packaging environments, those fibers are a contamination risk. They land on exposed product, on open-top containers, and on packaging surfaces. In regulated food facilities operating under FDA oversight, fiber contamination is a documented hazard that triggers corrective action.

A corrugated PP slip sheet has no paper, no pulp, and no fiber. The polypropylene surface is smooth and non-reactive. It does not shed particles under normal handling loads. It meets FDA 21 CFR requirements for polypropylene used in food-contact applications, making it an appropriate choice for produce packing houses, food processing distribution, beverage facilities, pharmaceutical packaging, and nutraceutical distribution — anywhere that fiber contamination from cardboard presents a real product safety concern.

Reuse Cycles and the Economics of PP vs. Cardboard

A single corrugated PP slip sheet is rated for 30 to 50 reuse cycles under normal handling conditions. That figure is not a marketing number — it is a function of the material properties of polypropylene and the structural design of the twin-wall profile. Polypropylene is impact resistant, flex-tolerant, and does not fatigue under repeated compressive loads the way paper fiber does.

When you calculate the cost per trip for a reusable PP slip sheet against the cost per trip of single-use cardboard tier sheets, the economics shift dramatically in favor of PP. The upfront unit cost of a PP slip sheet is higher than a cardboard tier sheet. But divided across 30 to 50 trips, the per-use cost of the PP sheet is a fraction of the per-use cost of cardboard. Add disposal costs, procurement overhead for constant cardboard reorders, and the labor involved in handling and compacting single-use waste, and the full financial picture strongly favors the transition to reusable PP slip sheets.

Standard Sizes, Custom Options, and Operational Fit

Elipacko supplies corrugated PP slip sheets in the two standard US pallet footprints — 40×48 inches for GMA-standard pallets and 48×48 inches for square pallet configurations — as well as custom-cut sizes for non-standard footprints. Whether you are running specialty export containers, automotive parts pallets, or retail display configurations that fall outside standard pallet dimensions, the PP corrugated slip sheet can be produced to your exact specification.

The lightweight nature of PP corrugated — a direct result of the enclosed air-channel construction — means there is no payload penalty for switching from cardboard to PP. In weight-sensitive shipping scenarios, such as air freight or LTL loads priced by weight, the switch to PP tier sheets can actually reduce per-shipment costs marginally by eliminating the accumulated weight of multiple cardboard layers.

Cold Chain Performance and Temperature Stability

Polypropylene maintains its mechanical properties across a wide temperature range. In refrigerated storage environments (typically 34–40°F) and frozen storage environments (0°F and below), PP corrugated slip sheets retain their dimensional stability and load-bearing capacity. Cardboard, by contrast, absorbs ambient moisture in refrigerated environments and softens, losing the structural rigidity that makes it useful as a tier divider. PP does not soften, does not absorb, and does not lose geometry in cold chain conditions.

For operations in produce cold storage, frozen food distribution, or pharmaceutical cold chain, this temperature stability is a direct operational advantage. You can build a pallet layer structure with PP slip sheets and trust that the structure will hold across temperature transitions — from the packing floor, through refrigerated transport, and into the receiving cold storage dock — without degradation of the tier sheet itself.

Sanitization, Compliance, and the FDA 21 CFR Standard

In food manufacturing and food distribution environments regulated under FDA guidelines, the materials that contact or are in proximity to food must meet defined safety criteria. For polypropylene, the relevant standard is FDA 21 CFR 177.1520, which specifies the conditions under which polypropylene resins are acceptable for use in articles that contact food. Elipacko's corrugated PP slip sheets are produced from polypropylene resin compounded to meet these requirements, providing a documented compliance basis for use in FDA-regulated food handling environments.

Beyond FDA compliance, the smooth non-porous PP surface supports effective sanitization protocols. The sheet can be cleaned with standard food-safe sanitizers, rinsed, and returned to use. This is not possible with cardboard, which cannot be sanitized and must be discarded after any significant contamination event. For facilities that operate under FSMA (Food Safety Modernization Act) preventive controls or that maintain GFSI-recognized certification programs, the ability to clean and document the sanitization of reusable packaging components is a meaningful compliance advantage.
